about us

Whether you're a beginner looking to learn scuba diving or an experienced diver seeking new underwater adventures, Fuerteventura offers a range of diving opportunities. Our school, Diving Atlantis, provides a safe and enjoyable experience for divers of all levels.


Located in front of the beach in La Lajita, our school offers a great location, well-cared-for equipment, and friendly, highly trained instructors who will guide you on the most exciting dives of Fuerteventura. scuba diving courses padi

Learn to dive safely and confidently!
PADI Open Water diver
No experience required

This is the most popular scuba diving course and serves as the entry point for beginners. It covers essential skills, knowledge, and safety procedures for scuba diving. The course includes e-learning (online theory), a confined water dive with a comprehensive set of exercises, 4 open water dives and a certification by PADI, the world’s most renowned scuba diving federation. Throughout the course, you will practice essential scuba skills under the guidance of our experienced instructor. Upon completing the Open Water Diver (OWD) course, you'll receive a certification that allows you to dive to a maximum depth of 18 meters in any country around the world.


If you don’t have enough time to complete full course, you can opt for the PADI Scuba Diver course, which is a shortened version of the OWD course, and takes only 1 day. Later on you can upgrade your license to Open Water Diver in any PADI dive center in the world. The Scuba Diver license allows you to dive down to 12 meters.


Experience the breathtaking beauty of the underwater world and become a certified diver!

The PADI Rescue Diver course teaches you how to prevent and effectively manage diving emergencies. padi divemaster

Divemaster is the first professional level in the PADI system of diver education. As a PADI Divemaster, you will acquire a high level of diving knowledge, skills, and experience, qualifying you to assist PADI instructors with dive courses and accompany certified divers on recreational dives. The Divemaster course involves knowledge development, practical application, and hands-on training. It covers topics such as dive theory, diving physics, physiology, equipment, environment, and dive planning. You will learn about dive leadership and how to supervise dive activities, assist with student divers, and conduct certain PADI programs. To complete the Divemaster course, you need to have 60 registered dives, and pass the final exams, which include written assessments, practical application evaluations, and a rescue assessment. Become a professional in the dive industry!


Additional costs: Divemaster materials (270 euros), and the PADI federation annual fee upon completing the course (about 125 euros).
